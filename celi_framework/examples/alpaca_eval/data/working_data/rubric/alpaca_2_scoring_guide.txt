# Alpaca Eval Response Grading Rubric

## 1. Accuracy (30 Points)
- **30**: Fully accurate, addressing the instruction correctly
- **20**: Mostly accurate with minor errors
- **10**: Significant inaccuracies affecting overall correctness
- **0**: Completely inaccurate or irrelevant

## 2. Relevance (25 Points)
- **25**: Directly addresses all aspects of the instruction
- **15**: Generally on-topic with some tangential content
- **10**: Minimally relevant, mostly off-topic
- **0**: Completely irrelevant to the instruction

## 3. Completeness (15 Points)
- **15**: Comprehensively answers all parts of the instruction
- **10**: Addresses most aspects, missing minor details
- **5**: Partially answers, omitting significant components
- **0**: Provides no meaningful information

## 4. Clarity and Structure (10 Points)
- **10**: Well-articulated and logically structured
- **7**: Mostly clear with minor structural issues
- **3**: Major clarity or structural problems
- **0**: Incoherent or incomprehensible

## 5. Conciseness (10 Points)
- **10**: Efficiently conveys information without excess
- **7**: Generally concise with some extraneous content
- **3**: Includes unnecessary details, diluting effectiveness
- **0**: Excessively verbose, detracting from core message

## 6. Creativity/Analytical Depth (10 Points)
- **10**: Exceptionally creative or insightful
- **7**: Shows some creativity or analytical depth
- **4**: Basic response without significant depth
- **0**: Lacks any creativity or analytical value

## Scoring Guidelines
- Provide specific examples to justify scores
- Consider context and complexity of instructions
- Sum category scores for final score out of 100

## Example
Instruction: "Name famous actors who started on Broadway"
High-scoring response: Lists relevant actors accurately, provides context, possibly includes interesting facts or insights
